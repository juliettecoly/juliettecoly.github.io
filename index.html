Juliette Coly

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Economics at Stanford University. My research focuses on spatial economics, economic history, and political economy, using rigorous econometric methods and machine learning.

Recent activities:

I wrote a short essay on economic tools that could be used to mitigate the environmental impact of data centers as part of the Center for AI Safety (CAIS) Winter 2026 course on AI Safety and Ethics.

I am TA-ing and designing part of the econometrics PhD-level class taught by Prof. Kevin Chen, which covers the theoretical foundations of econometric identification and inference, with an emphasis on asymptotic theory and estimation methods.

I am volunteering as a tour guide at the Stanford Cantor Museum and Anderson Collection.
